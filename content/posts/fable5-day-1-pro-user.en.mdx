# Fable 5 access email sat for 3 days — here's what happened

> **Site disclosure**: This post is an independent third-party test. The author is a Claude Pro user with no official affiliation with Anthropic. Nothing here constitutes an official endorsement or purchasing advice.

---

The Fable 5 access email sat in my inbox for three days.

I saw it. I just didn't dare open it.

## The email I didn't open

On the evening of June 10, Anthropic released two new models — **Fable 5** and **Mythos 5**. I wrote about it that night. That post was the view from outside.

Three days later, the email arrived. The subject was something like "Your access to Fable 5 is now open." I read it three times. I didn't click.

Not paranoia. Real fear.

Every time Claude pushes a major update, I rush to upgrade and crash into a half-finished product: API unstable, terminal disconnects, context resets mid-task. The worst one was the night Sonnet 4.5 launched — I rewrote my skill files three times.

So this email sat for three days.

## Terminal test: even Opus 4.8 is gone

Last night I finally cracked and ran an old script in the terminal.

Error:

```
Error: Model 'claude-opus-4-8' not found.
You may have access to claude-fable-5.
```

I stared at the screen for ten seconds.

Opus 4.8 — the model I'd used for two months, the core of my workflow — **was gone**. Not slower. Removed from the menu entirely.

My first reaction: I've been robbed. I'm paying Anthropic for Pro, and the tool I depend on got cut, replaced by a new model I haven't learned yet.

You know that feeling? Like when your phone's most-used app gets removed and replaced with a new version that looks the same but works completely differently.

## Reddit was on fire

I went to Reddit's r/Claude to see if anyone else was dealing with this.

No need to scroll past the first page.

Top post: "Fable 5 dropped, Opus 4.8 is gone, anyone else?"

Comments, all in the same vein:

- "Same here, terminal doesn't recognize it."
- "Anthropic should have grandfathered existing users."
- "I'm paying $20/month to beta test their new model."

I scrolled for dozens more.

**Not a single one said "it works."**

For a moment I figured the email was a mistake — either Anthropic sent it to the wrong account, or my permission was misconfigured.

## I almost gave up

I opened the Claude app to dismiss the red banner.

But my finger slipped and tapped the tab on the left.

Not Chat. **Co-Work.**

Co-Work is the new mode in Anthropic's desktop app — I hadn't used it before, but I'd heard it lets Claude take over the screen and run tasks.

I switched over.

The input box was simpler than Chat's. No model selector. No attachment button. Just one line: **Describe what you want done.**

With nothing to lose, I pasted the description of that big task I'd been holding back.

Pressed Enter.

**The green light came on.**

Not a delayed spinner. Real progress. The bar started moving.

I watched for thirty seconds — it was reading my files, calling my tools, walking step by step through the skill docs I'd written months ago.

I have to admit it —

**I got hooked.**

## Pro users actually got access

What surprised me most wasn't that it worked — it was that **it worked for me.**

I'm a Pro user. $20/month. Before Fable 5, every top-tier model was either inaccessible to Pro accounts or limited to lightweight tasks.

But this time, it really was running my big task.

The skill and SOP docs I'd configured for Fable 5 — automation scripts that walk through dozens of steps — were actually executing inside Co-Work.

I realized: Anthropic didn't just rename a model. They rebuilt the question of "can Pro users run long tasks?"

There was a line in the email I hadn't read carefully: **Available through June 22, 2026 as part of your plan.**

Until June 22, my Pro plan can run it.

## I gave it a big task

I didn't sleep last night.

Not because I was worried. Because it **was actually running.**

That big task I mentioned —

(You know how it is — write the specifics and you spoil tomorrow's full review.)

In short: I'd built a skill workflow that walks Claude step-by-step through an SOP. I'd run it on Opus 4.8 a few times. It worked, but I had to watch every step, and it'd disconnect every few hours.

Last night, Fable 5 ran for **5 hours straight** without disconnecting.

**Five hours.**

For a Pro user, that's more than half a month's quota compressed into one task.

My first reaction after —

"All those nights I worked overtime, could I have done it this way?"

## I can't publish a full review yet, but I can tell you a few things now

By rights, I should publish a full review tonight. I'm not going to.

Reason: Fable 5 has only run for 5 hours at my end. One successful run doesn't count. I need to watch it through **real multi-day continuous tasks** to see if it breaks, gets lazy, or suddenly goes dumb.

And June 22 is a hard cutoff — if the permission gets pulled back, I'll have nothing left to test.

So I'm giving myself **the entire window**.

But a few things I can tell you right now:

**One — don't delay the email.**

I sat on this email for three days and missed what? Nothing. Fable 5 access opened the night of launch; no sooner, no later. But if you're also waiting — try it.

**Two — don't panic at terminal errors.**

Opus 4.8 is genuinely gone. If your local client doesn't have Fable 5 on the menu, it'll throw "model not found." That's not a bug — Anthropic pushed the version too hard and didn't give legacy users a transition.

**Three — try the Co-Work mode in the Claude app.**

This was my biggest find last night. Just because the terminal doesn't work doesn't mean it doesn't work. Co-Work mode takes over directly and handles long tasks.

**Four — Pro users really can run big tasks.**

I didn't dare to expect this. Fable 5 cracked the door open.
