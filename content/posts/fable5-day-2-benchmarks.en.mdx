# Fable 5 Day 2: 9 hours without a break, but one overhyped feature

> **Disclaimer**: This is an independent third-party field test. The author is a Claude Pro user with no official affiliation with Anthropic. Nothing here constitutes official endorsement or financial advice.

---

When I closed Day 1 with "it really runs", Fable 5 was still going.

Day 2 morning I woke up and went straight to Co-Work mode to check the progress bar.

**It had finished.**

Total runtime: 9 hours 14 minutes.

I had hit 5 hours the previous night. This morning at 11:00 I started a second run; that one also finished cleanly by 13:00.

Two back-to-back long runs. **Neither one dropped.**

That wasn't possible with Opus 4.8. Past 3 hours on Opus 4.8 meant I had to babysit it, and I lost two sessions to disconnects that required manual reconnection.

## Real numbers: 9-hour completion vs my expectations

My task is a multi-stage skill pipeline: read files → modify code → run tests → fix → commit → start the next cycle.

I'll spare the step count (it's my workflow), but the task has 7 stages. Fable 5 powered through stages 1-4 without hesitation. On stage 5 it caught an edge case I hadn't seen before and added a guard. After stage 6 I told it to sleep for 30 minutes to see if it would self-resume — it didn't, I had to manually send "continue" before it picked up stage 7.

**That exposed Fable 5's first real truth:**

It has no real "wait" mechanism.

It can run for 9 hours, but if you put it to sleep, **it does not wake up on its own.** You have to push it manually.

Opus 4.8, in contrast, would poll on its own after a sleep and self-resume within 1-2 minutes. Fable 5 does not.

This matters. **Every claim that "Fable 5 runs long tasks unattended" is overstated.** You still have to monitor it; the cadence goes from "every 30 minutes" to "every 3 hours", that's all.

## Speed benchmark: 30% faster than Opus 4.8, 15% slower than Sonnet 4.5

I ran the same code task (an 800-line refactor) through all three models:

| Task | Fable 5 | Opus 4.8 | Sonnet 4.5 |
|------|---------|----------|------------|
| 800-line refactor | 4m 12s | 6m 03s | 3m 38s |
| Cross-file 5-file bug fix | 2m 41s | 3m 55s | 2m 17s |
| 120K-token long-context summary | 58s | 1m 32s | 1m 14s |

Fable 5 is roughly 30% faster than Opus 4.8 on average. **That I'll concede.**

Against Sonnet 4.5, Fable 5 is actually **15% slower.**

I ran it three times. The gap holds at 12-18%.

This tells me one thing: **Fable 5 is not a Sonnet replacement, it's an Opus replacement.** If you want speed, Sonnet 4.5 is still king. If you want long-horizon capability, Fable 5 is the new ceiling.

## Context overflow: where I almost crashed

**This is the part of Day 2 worth writing about.**

Halfway through stage 6 of the second run, Co-Work mode threw this:

```
[Context overflow] Session has used 198k of 200k tokens.
Continuing this task would lose earlier context. 
Suggested: compact now, or fork session.
```

On Opus 4.8 I never hit this running 9-hour tasks because Opus 4.8 hits the same 200K ceiling but takes much longer to get there.

**But Fable 5 is fast, and it eats tokens fast.**

I told Co-Work to "compact now."

It did, took 5 minutes, and resumed the task. The first 6 stages of dialogue were compressed into a 4K summary.

**The task did not drop.**

That is its **real killer feature.**

On Opus 4.8, when context fills up, you fork the session and lose the first 6 stages of work. Fable 5's Co-Work has a built-in compact mechanism I didn't know about — it summarises old dialogue and **continues the new task in the same session.**

**That was the moment I realized: Fable 5's "long-horizon ability" is not "it can run longer", it's "it has a compact mechanism that lets it keep going."**

I dug around on Reddit afterwards. This compact mechanism shipped June 1st, but **the official announcement never mentioned it.** It doesn't make headlines. In practice it's a lifesaver.

## Pro user quota: didn't reset on day 2, but...

On Day 1 I said there's a 5-hour quota reset mechanism. I have to **correct that** on Day 2.

I started a second task at 11:00 the next morning. **It didn't ask me to pay anything. It just started running.**

But at 14:00 I checked the dashboard:

```
Session usage: 4.2 hours of 30h monthly cap.
```

**It billed my 5-hour quota directly to the monthly cap.**

The 5-hour window isn't "5 hours then forced sleep" — it's "5 hours on the Pro plan, **then bill against the 30h monthly cap**."

Monthly cap: 30 hours. I've used 9.

**Remaining budget through June 22: 21 hours.**

What I said on Day 1 — "Fable 5 runs freely on Pro" — I take that back.

It can run, but **it is not free.** Pro just lowers the bar from $200/month to $20/month. **There's still a monthly ceiling.**

## My new workflow: Fable 5 + Sonnet 4.5 split

I thought at the end of Day 1 that Fable 5 is the "run everything" model. After Day 2 I have a new split:

| Task type | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Short-and-fast (< 1 hour) | Sonnet 4.5 (faster, cheaper) |
| Long-horizon (> 1 hour, multi-stage) | Fable 5 (compact + endurance) |
| Heavy reasoning (math, deep code analysis) | Opus 4.8 (removed from menu, API still works) |

Fable 5 does not replace Sonnet 4.5. **It replaces Opus 4.8's position in long-horizon scenarios.**

## Where I land on Fable 5 right now

Two days in, I can't make a final call. But here are the things **I can already confirm:**

**Confirmed:**
- ✅ Runs 9+ hour long tasks (compact mechanism is the key)
- ✅ ~30% faster than Opus 4.8
- ✅ Pro users really can use it, but the 5h cap flows into a 30h monthly ceiling
- ✅ "Available through June 22" in the email is real, not marketing copy
- ❌ No auto sleep-wake; manual push required
- ❌ 15% slower than Sonnet 4.5; not suited to short-and-fast

**Still uncertain:**
- ❓ Can state be reused across sessions? (If yes, Pro workflows can be redesigned)
- ❓ Does it degrade in real production loads? (Haven't hit it, but my sample is still small)
- ❓ What happens after June 22? (Anthropic hasn't said)
